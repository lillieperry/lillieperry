Goggles

Goggles coming off when you swim is a common problem. However, their are a couple things you can do to help prevent this from happening while you are swimming. First, make sure you goggles fit! Do the goggle test. Stick the lens of your goggles on your face, if they stick for a second, it is usually a good fit. Also, make sure that they are on tight. If they are not tight enough, they will not be suctioned to your face, so they will leek water and/or fall off. Last, you can wear two swim caps to keep them on. Put one swim cap on your head, position your goggles on over your cap, and then put your second swim cap on over your goggles. This should help prevent them from falling off.

Swimmer's Ear

Swimmer's ear is when water remains in the outer ear canal after swimming. This creates a moist emviroment that can cause bacteria and fungi to grow and an infeection to develop. The best way to prevent an infection is to prevent swimmer's ear. Use swimmer's ear eardrops or alchohal to dry the water that remains in your ear. Also, you can wear a swim cap that covers your ears, and wear ear plugs. If an infection does develop and it does not improve, you may want to talk to your doctor. Preventing swimmer's ear is very important, and it can keep an infection from developing!
